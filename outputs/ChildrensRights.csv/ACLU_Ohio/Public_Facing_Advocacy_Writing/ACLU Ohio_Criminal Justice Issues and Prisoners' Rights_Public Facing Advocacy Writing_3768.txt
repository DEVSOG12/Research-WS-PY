TVs, radios, and reading materials may or may not be available. Visits from family are strictly limited, if permitted at all. Almost all human contact occurs while in restraints or through a barrier.This is life in solitary confinement.It’s life for approximately 80,000 adults and 70,000 children across the country held in solitary every day.Individuals placed in solitary confinement exhibit a variety of negative psychological reactions, including severe and chronic depression; paranoia; delusions; and irrational rage. Self-mutilation is a common practice, and suicide rates are higher.Learn more, by reading our Civil Liberties Briefing: Locked Up, Locked Out: Solitary Confinement in Ohio.Solitary confinement is a costly concept in many ways. The human costs of solitary confinement are extremely high for those who experience it. There are monetary costs for society as well, since building and staffing isolation units are more expensive than traditional prisons. Additionally, recidivism rates are higher for those who’ve spent time in solitary, leading to larger prison populations.There are better alternatives to using solitary confinement. At minimum, Ohio’s prisons should adopt the American Bar Association’s Standards for Criminal Justice, Treatment of Prisoners, which, among other things, call for appropriate procedures for the use of solitary; limits to time spent in solitary; and ending solitary confinement for those with mental illness.Check out our new fact sheet to learn more about the impact solitary confinement has on those who experience it, their families, and the larger community.Stay informedEmail address *Zip code *Leave this field blankJoin our Action TeamStand with usRelated IssuesDisability RightsJuvenile JusticeDocumentspdf2014_06SolitaryConfinement-CivilLibertiesBriefing.pdfpdf2014_06SolitaryConfinement-CivilLibertiesBriefing.pdfRelated contentWhen Does Childhood End?September 26, 2022New Data Shows Over-Policing, Racial Disparities, and Lack of...July 13, 2022Policing in Cincinnati Public Schools: Community Survey ResultsJuly 13, 2022Disparate Racial Discipline In Cincinnati Public SchoolsJuly 13, 2022Ending the School-To-Prison Pipeline: A Case Study of Cincinnati...June 24, 2021Statehouse Republicans Have Approved Radical Changes to Ohio Budget...June 11, 2021Alarming New Data Shows Over-Policing, Racial Disparities, and Lack...April 22, 2021Introducing Our Columbus Police Contract Negotiation ToolkitMarch 12, 2021Dear Cleveland, We Need Bail Reform.
