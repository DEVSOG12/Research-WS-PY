People who cannot pay risk a spiraling set of consequences—such as losing their driver’s licenses—and ultimately owing even more money if their debt is sent to private debt collectors. However, for all the harm caused, revenue collected through fines and fees typically make up a tiny fraction of government budgets.This series of research reports details the numerous fines and fees levied by government bodies in Florida, New Mexico, New York, Virginia, and Washington State—revealing the cumulative toll they take on people.
