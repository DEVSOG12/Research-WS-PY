This report fills a gap until the Bureau of Justice Statistics releases its next annual report—likely in early 2021. Vera collected updated data on people in prison at the end of the first quarter of 2020 to reflect any changes that had occurred as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. Population data collected from 44 states and the Federal Bureau of Prisons (BOP) in response to the COVID-19 pandemic showed only a 1.6 percent decrease during the first three months of 2020.Publication HighlightsThe prison incarceration rate at the end of 2019 was 437 people in prison per 100,000 residents, a 2.6 percent drop from 2018.Data from 44 states and the BOP show that no system had moved with the urgency required to meet the recommendations of public health officials to reduce incarceration in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.Between year-end 2018 and year-end 2019, prison incarceration rates fell in 41 states and grew in nine others.Key FactsIn 2019 there were1,260,400people under state prison jurisdiction, 28,200 fewer than in 2018.The prison incarceration rate has dropped17.5%since its peak in 2007.The number of people in prison increased in11 statesin 2019: Alabama, Alaska, Arizona, Georgia, Idaho, Indiana, Nebraska, North Dakota, Montana, Utah, and West Virginia.RelatedPUBLICATIONPeople in Prison in 2018Apr 23, 2019SPOTLIGHTCriminal Justice and the COVID-19 PandemicMay 06, 2020NEWSUse this Data to Hold Your Local Jail Accountable During the PandemicApr 08, 202034 35th StreetSuite 4-2ABrooklyn, NY 11232212-334-1300About UsCareersEventsMediaContact UsKeep up with us on social media.Transformative change, sent to your inbox.Add your email© Vera Institute of Justice.
