In 2019, a typical New York county spent more than $225 to incarcerate a single person for a single night, or more than $82,000 per year. In fact, jail budgets have grown even as the number of people in jail has gone down. Every dollar spent on the jail comes at the expense of funding other services that could help to build healthy and safe communities.
