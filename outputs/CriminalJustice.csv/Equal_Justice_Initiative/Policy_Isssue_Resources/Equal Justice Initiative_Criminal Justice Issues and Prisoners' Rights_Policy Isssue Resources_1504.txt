The numbers represent the largest spike in Covid cases to date in Alabama prisons, al.com reports.ADOC said it tested everyone incarcerated at Elmore Correctional Facility who was not showing symptoms on August 11 after a recent increase in cases there. Out of 960 people tested, 191 new cases—20%—were identified at Elmore alone.Eleven incarcerated people tested positive at Draper Quarantine Intake Facility and there was one new positive case at Easterling Correctional Facility in Clio, Alabama, bringing the statewide total to 203 new positive cases as of August 13.ADOC also reported it has 224 active Covid-19 cases as of last Friday, which represents a dramatic increase from 22 active cases on August 6.Sixty-six incarcerated people and three employees have died after testing positive for Covid-19 so far, according to ADOC’s public dashboard.Tags:AlabamaPrison ConditionsRelated ResourcesCovid-19 Cases Spike at Alabama's Elmore Prison; 20% Test Positiveal.comRecent NewsMore newsEJI Releases New Report on the Transatlantic Slave Trade10.25.22Daniel Smith, Believed to Be the Last Child of Enslaved People, Dies at 9010.21.22Homicide at Limestone Is 16th in Alabama Prisons This Year10.18.22EJI Partners Across the Country Dedicate Markers Recognizing Lynchings10.14.22More newsFollow EJIfacebooktwitterinstagramyoutubeHomeMuseum and MemorialNewsAboutContactGet InvolvedDonateShop122 Commerce Street Montgomery, AL 36104(334) 269-1803[email protected]Subscribe to our newsletter© Equal Justice Initiative 2022
