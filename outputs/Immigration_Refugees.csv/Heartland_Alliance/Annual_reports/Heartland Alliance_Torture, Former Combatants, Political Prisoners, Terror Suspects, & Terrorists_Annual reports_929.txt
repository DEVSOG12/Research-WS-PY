Annual Report 2007Transforming lives. Transforming communities.Transformation. It is a powerful journey: One that can lift the spirit. 
 Help realize human potential. Create a world of justice and opportunity.  At Heartland Alliance for Human 
Needs & Human Rights, transforma-tion is what drives us. It underlies every day of our work helping thou-sands of men, women, and children threatened by poverty and danger move from crisis to stability and on to success.
Whether it is our work in housing, 
health care, legal protections, or economic security, we transform lives.  We transform communities. 
Heartland Alliance has spurred 
many exciting transformations over the past year:
Once pock-marked high-rise public 
housing buildings are now vibrant mixed-income communities. Young people anxious for basic health care can now access desperately need-ed dental services at our school-based health centers. Homeless families struggling to stand on their own are on the path to economic stability and security. And immigrants unjustly detained through a broken immigration system gain access to critical legal services to help reunite them with their families.
But transformation is not just our 
end. It is also our means. As an organization, Heartland Alliance has transformed to address evolving societal challenges. We increasingly share and apply our knowledge to help other communities address similar problems. We use our 119 years of experience providing human services with growing frequency to make a lasting impact at the policy making level. 
These transformations will ultimately 
carry us to our destination of a more just society where the human rights of all are fully realized. Thank you for your passion and investment in this important journey.

 participant in Heartland Alliance’s Transitional   Jobs program.A Key, a Door, a Lock.  Most of us take for granted the weight 
of a key in our pocket, the slam of a door at the end of the 
day, or the click of the lock as we secure our homes for the night. These are potent symbols of an elusive dream when you’re struggling to ﬁnd or maintain a home. Hav-ing a roof over your head is not just a fundamental need, but a basic human right. Heartland Alliance knows that having a space to call home, a place to develop roots, pro-vides purpose and possibility—a springboard to a brighter future. 
Transforming Communities. Nowhere is this more apparent 
than among residents of the Chicago Housing Authority, one of the nation’s largest and oldest public housing agen-cies. Heartland Alliance has worked as a partner with the Chicago Housing Authority to help transform Chicago’s public housing—deep, concentrated pockets of urban pov-
erty and decline—into healthy, vibrant communities.Transforming lives and communities through  housing .
We’ve taken our expertise in successfully developing more than 1,000 affordable homes to create revitalized, mixed-income communities with the CHA in Chicago neighborhoods on the near west and south sides. This year alone, we developed more than 220 affordable resi-dences at the Roosevelt Square and Jazz on the Boulevard housing developments. 
But we know that a stable home takes more than just 
four walls and a roof—it means having opportunities to be a successful member of your community. That’s why Heartland Alliance provides CHA residents with life skills and tools—such as innovative job training pro-grams, employment referrals, and a “101” on building a nest egg. 
Other cities in the Midwest have turned to Heartland Al-
liance to learn from this recipe of success. Our next fron-tier: helping the cities of Milwaukee, Wisc. and Benton Harbor, Mich. tackle similar challenges in their communities.

Alone, without access to a lawyer.  Held indeﬁnitely with- 
out a day in court. Inhumanely treated, many times 
without basic, sometimes life-saving, medical care. It’s the story of more than a quarter million immigrants—men, women, and children—detained in a complex web of county jails and federal facilities. Most pose no threat to our communities. Many are ﬂeeing persecution and seek-ing asylum. It’s a system that tramples the cornerstones of our U.S. justice system. Undermining our moral and democratic values. Threatening the rights of us all.  
Transforming a Broken Immigration System. Heartland Alliance 
believes a common-sense approach must trump this warehousing of people in the U.S. immigrant detention system. Through our National Immigrant Justice Center, Heartland Alliance is using precedent-setting legal cases, Transforming lives and communities through  legal protections .
public education, and advocacy to help restore a standard of basic human decency to the immigrant detention system.
This past year, we helped put detention reform on the 
federal legislative map—encouraging the “Secure and Safe Detention and Asylum Act”—during the U.S. Sen-ate’s immigration reform efforts.  We have educated 1,200 detainees about their basic rights, secured the release of more than 150 detainees, and provided more than 7,500 people with legal services in the past year.   
While immigration reform did not cross the legislative ﬁn-
ish line this year, Heartland Alliance remains committed to reforming this inhumane and unjust system of detaining people. This commitment is the fabric of who we are and what we do—restoring hope and the promise of the Ameri-can Dream for all.

Transforming Lives. Fleeing violence at home. Stranded 
at an emergency shelter, children in hand. Scared, and no place else to go. This is the disheartening reality of many of Heartland Alliance’s participants in its Families Building Community program. The program helps home-less families, many of whom have experienced domestic violence or substance abuse, embark on the road to eco-nomic self-sufﬁciency. Transforming lives and communities through  economic security .
Heartland Alliance works closely with program partici-pants over a one-year period, placing them in safe apart-ments with leases held in the participant’s name—a unique program feature that helps participants develop a ﬁnancial track record from day one. Heartland Alliance provides intensive supportive services—ranging from job search assistance to domestic violence counseling to learning the basics of budgeting—a comprehensive ap-proach that creates the critical stepping stones to a safer and more secure future. 
The program works. This year, Families Building Community 
enters its 15th year, having helped more than 500 fami-lies. An extraordinary 87 percent of recent program graduates remain housed one year later. Among these families, those completing a one-year follow up showed a 97 percent increase in their income from the time they started the program. It’s all part of Heartland Alliance’s informed expertise—creating paths from crisis to stability and on to success.
