The past year included many extraordinary 
achievements for Heartland Alliance, despite 
the ongoing and growing challenges in the world 
around us. Because of key donors and partners 
like you, our work both with individuals and on 
systemic levels is making a real difference. 
As you’ll read in this report, our work remains 
on the cutting edge, is being affirmed by 
important leaders and funders, and is upholding 
the 127-year Heartland Alliance tradition of 
innovation and impact that changes lives here in 
the Midwest and around the world. 
Thank you for your support and partnership in 
creating opportunity for those seeking home, 
safety, and justice.

We created a new opportunity for 
the formerly incarcerated to find 
stable ground 
The University of Chicago Urban Labs awarded 
us a $1 million innovation challenge grant to, 
in partnership with Treatment Alternatives 
for Safe Communities (TASC), build centers 
tailored to the needs of those who are leaving 
jail and experiencing mental illness.HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  9
We led the way in researching the 
oral health and obesity rates of 
Illinois children 
Our Oral Health Forum organized a statewide 
evaluation of 3,800 Illinois third-graders and 
found that, while rates of tooth decay and 
obesity are decreasing, minority and  
low-income Illinois children experience 
significant disparities in both of these crucial 
health issues. HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  10
We helped 
immigrants 
find safety and 
justice HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  11
We secured improved protections 
for transgender immigrants
Our National Immigrant Justice Center, in 
collaboration with other LGBT advocates, 
argued before a federal appeals court on the 
need to ensure due process for transgender 
immigrants seeking protection in the U.S., 
preventing the deportation of a number of 
transgender women whose lives would be in 
danger should they be forced to return home.HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  12
We ended practices that kept 
people unlawfully detained for 
prolonged periods
Through a nationwide class action lawsuit 
and after years of advocacy, our National 
Immigrant Justice Center and its partners 
reached a groundbreaking settlement 
on behalf of individuals in Department of 
Homeland Security custody with final removal 
orders who fear persecution or torture in their 
home countries. HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  13
Often held for months while waiting for 
screening interviews to determine if they 
will be allowed to seek protection in the U.S., 
these individuals will now be screened within 
10 days, an important step to ensure that the 
U.S. government complies with its obligations 
under international law to protect refugees.HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  14
We helped refugees and 
immigrants escaping violence

We welcomed 321 arriving refugees and

build stable, safe, healthy lives here, offering

We offered healing and justice to 
Syrians in crisis 
As millions have fled Syria for Lebanon, we 
have responded to violence against the most 
vulnerable — women and girls. To date we’ve 
served thousands through mental and physical 
health care, legal services, vocational training, 
and child care services, all while advocating at 
the highest levels for additional protection.  HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  17
We led a new approach to  
mental healthcare 
In the Democratic Republic of Congo, where 
internal conflict has left many suffering 
physical, mental, and emotional trauma, 
mental healthcare services are nearly 
nonexistent. Working closely with the 
government, we led a program to train and 
place mental healthcare professionals in 
the primary care system, treating trauma 
survivors as they rebuild their lives.HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  18

We got a big win for Illinois 
workers — the Illinois Secure 
Choice Savings Program 
This program — the first of its kind in 
the nation — gives workers access to an 
employment-based retirement savings 
account so they have more than just Social 
Security to rely on. HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  20
We grew jobs — literally
We quadrupled the size of our urban farm, 
allowing us to grow 34,000 servings of 
vegetables and provide job-training to 34 
people in food warehousing, distribution and 
farming, training them for in-demand jobs in 
their community.HEARTLAND ALLIANCE  |  2015 ANNUAL REPORT  |  SUPPORTERS  |  21
We helped those
